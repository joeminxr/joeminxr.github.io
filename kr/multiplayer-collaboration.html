원자력 발전소
XR 협업 훈련
As the Lead Engineer at Systems Technology Inc., I designed and delivered XR Training solutions to address a critical challenge within the nuclear industry: generational knowledge gap. With seasoned technicians retiring at an accelerated rate, capturing and transferring their expertise became paramount.

Leveraging Unity, I developed multi-platform training tools deployed across PCVR, Mobile VR, AR headsets, and tablets. These tools provide an interactive, hands-on way to train the next generation of technicians, bridging the knowledge gap and elevating the quality of education.

Through XR Training, students could engage with high-risk scenarios in a no-risk environment. From handling complex systems to simulating emergency procedures, the immersive learning experiences ensured they gained critical skills without compromising safety—offering an innovative solution to train confident, capable technicians for the future of the nuclear industry.


액센츄어
몰입형 협업 플랫폼
At Accenture, I contributed to the development of their Immersive Collaboration Platform, an in-house social and collaborative XR platform designed to bring teams together in a more personal and engaging way compared to traditional video conferencing. Similar in concept to Microsoft’s AltspaceVR, this platform aimed to redefine how teams connect virtually.

My contributions focused on implementing critical features to enhance usability and functionality. I integrated AWS services, including IAM for secure user authentication and DynamoDB for real-time data storage and retrieval. I developed private rooms with robust access control mechanisms, allowing users to collaborate securely, and built an invitation system for seamless user onboarding. Additionally, I implemented a spectator view, enabling non-XR-participating users to observe sessions without disrupting the experience, utilizing real-time rendering optimizations.


GigXR
Remote Rendering 통합
During my time at Accenture, I collaborated with GigXR to explore solutions for optimizing the performance of their multi-user platform. Our goal was to enhance stability and create a smoother experience by addressing the rendering limitations of AR devices.

We developed a prototype built on their existing framework, integrating Remote Rendering to offload resource-intensive processes from the device. By combining remotely rendered entities with on-device rendered entities, we achieved a balanced approach that significantly reduced rendering overhead while maintaining high-quality visuals.

This prototype demonstrated the potential for scalable, high-performance multi-user experiences in AR environments.
